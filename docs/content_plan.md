# Content Plan — Coverage Against the July 2026 ECO

Tracks how the existing question/lesson bank maps onto the current PMP
Exam Content Outline (effective July 9, 2026), which consolidated the
retired 2021 ECO from 35 tasks to 26 (8 People, 10 Process, 8 Business
Environment). See `decision_log.md` Decision #10 for the remap rationale
and the domain/task moves.

Counts below are taken from the actual entries in `data/questions.json`
and `data/lessons.json` — not estimated.

## Coverage status

- **Every one of the 26 ECO tasks has at least one question.** (Question
  breadth: complete.)
- **Business Environment now has full lesson coverage** — all 8 tasks have
  a lesson, up from zero.
- **6 tasks still have no lesson:** People Tasks 3, 4, 8 and Process Tasks
  2, 5, 7. These remain the open breadth gap on the lesson side (see Open
  items). Full "a lesson AND a question for every task" is therefore **not
  yet** reached — 20 of 26 tasks have both.

## Domain weighting (current, July 2026 ECO)

| Domain | Exam weight | Tasks | Questions | Lessons |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| People | 33% | 8 | 11 | 5 |
| Process | 41% | 10 | 11 | 7 |
| Business Environment | 26% | 8 | 9 | 8 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **26** | **31** | **20** |

Business Environment carries roughly a quarter of the exam (26%), not
one-twelfth (8%) as under the 2021 ECO. Its earlier zero-lesson gap is now
closed.

## Status legend

- **Q+L** — has at least one question and one lesson
- **Questions only** — questions but no lesson
- **Lesson only** — a lesson but no question (none remain)
- **No content** — nothing yet (none remain)

## Open items

- **Lesson gaps (6 tasks):** People Tasks 3, 4, 8 and Process Tasks 2, 5,
  7 are Questions-only. Adding a lesson to each closes the last of the
  breadth gaps and brings all 26 tasks to Q+L.
- **Question depth:** most tasks sit at a single question (People Task 3
  has 4; Process Task 3 and Business Environment Task 4 have 2). Deepening
  to multiple questions per task strengthens quiz variety — a Phase 2
  concern, not a breadth blocker.
