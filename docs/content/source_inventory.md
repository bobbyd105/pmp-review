# PMP Source Library Inventory

## Scope and handling

This inventory covers all 11 local PDF references under `docs/Source/`. The files are local-only research inputs, are excluded from Git, and are not curriculum artifacts. Descriptions below are original summaries derived from the source structure and topic coverage; they do not reproduce slide text.

The two local planning maps—`PMP_Master_Curriculum_and_Source_Map.md` and `PMP_Repo_Curriculum_Coverage_Crosswalk.md`—were also reviewed as prior analysis. They are useful hypotheses, not authoritative repository documentation. Repository reality and machine checks take precedence where they differ.

## Inventory

### S1 — PMBOK 8 process chart

- **File:** `00_PMBOK+8+Process+Chart.pdf` (2 pages)
- **Purpose:** Compact orientation to the seven performance domains, five focus areas, and process placement.
- **Major concepts:** Domain/focus-area matrix; process names; structural relationships.
- **Unique material:** The most concise visual index of the 40-process model.
- **Overlap:** Summarized by S5 and explained process-by-process in S8.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** PMBOK 8 process matrix; no formula treatment.
- **Terminology:** Performance domain, focus area, process.
- **Recommended role:** Structural authority and reference-sheet source; not a stand-alone lesson.

### S2 — Project management terms

- **File:** `03_PM+Terms (1).pdf` (33 pages)
- **Purpose:** Foundation vocabulary and high-level distinctions.
- **Major concepts:** Projects, value, programs, portfolios, operations, phases, life cycles, governance, stakeholders, PMO types, organizational structures, constraints, product versus project management, risks versus issues, leadership, and introductory AI.
- **Unique material:** Broadest beginner-friendly terminology bridge into the rest of the library.
- **Overlap:** Principles in S3, life cycles in S4, domains in S5, leadership in S9, and AI in S11.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** PMO authority types and organizational structure comparisons; no substantive formula set.
- **Terminology:** Core nouns and role distinctions used across every module.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for Foundations and Glossary; secondary source elsewhere.

### S3 — Project management principles

- **File:** `04_PM+Principles.pdf` (25 pages)
- **Purpose:** Behavioral and decision-making foundation.
- **Major concepts:** Holistic thinking, value, embedded quality, accountable leadership, sustainability, and empowered culture.
- **Unique material:** Organizes the exam mindset into six connected principles rather than isolated rules.
- **Overlap:** Value and leadership recur in S2, S5, S9, S10, and S11.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Six-principle model; proactive, ownership, and value-driven mindset dimensions.
- **Terminology:** Value, accountability, empowerment, sustainability, systems/holistic view.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for Principles & Mindset and explanation-quality standards.

### S4 — Project management approaches

- **File:** `05_PM+Approaches.pdf` (24 pages)
- **Purpose:** Explain life cycles, development approaches, focus areas, and tailoring.
- **Major concepts:** Phases, phase gates, changing risk/cost patterns, predictive, adaptive, hybrid, and focus-area intent.
- **Unique material:** Clean separation of project life cycle from product development approach.
- **Overlap:** Foundation definitions in S2; detailed predictive processes in S8; adaptive and hybrid mechanics in S9/S10.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Approach-selection and tailoring criteria; no calculation focus.
- **Terminology:** Predictive, adaptive, iterative, incremental, hybrid, phase gate, tailoring.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for the course sequence between Foundations and domain/process depth.

### S5 — Project performance domains

- **File:** `06_Project+Performance+Domains.pdf` (25 pages)
- **Purpose:** Domain-level overview and integration view.
- **Major concepts:** Governance, scope, schedule, finance, stakeholders, resources, risk, their interactions, and useful indicators.
- **Unique material:** Describes what good performance looks like at the domain level without becoming an ITTO catalog.
- **Overlap:** S1 provides placement; S8 supplies process detail.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Seven-domain model; risk appetite/tolerance concepts.
- **Terminology:** Performance outcome, indicator, governance, domain interactions.
- **Recommended role:** Orientation lessons and cross-domain synthesis.

### S6 — Common ITTOs

- **File:** `07_Common+ITTOs.pdf` (23 pages)
- **Purpose:** Teach reusable process mechanics without rote memorization.
- **Major concepts:** EEFs, OPAs, project documents, project management plan, expert judgment, data gathering/analysis/representation, decisions, interpersonal skills, meetings, change requests, work performance data/information/reports, and updates.
- **Unique material:** Pattern-based ITTO instruction and critical document distinctions.
- **Overlap:** S8 supplies process-specific examples.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Data → information → reports; recurring input/tool/output categories.
- **Terminology:** EEF, OPA, PM plan, project document, change request, work performance artifacts.
- **Recommended role:** Shared reference layer embedded across process lessons.

### S7 — AI template prompt

- **File:** `08.1_AI+Prompt+to+use+for+template.pdf` (1 page)
- **Purpose:** Operational prompt pattern for completing project-management templates.
- **Major concepts:** Prompt inputs, contextual constraints, template-oriented outputs, and review expectations.
- **Unique material:** A reusable authoring/prompt-helper asset rather than exam curriculum.
- **Overlap:** Responsible use and AI capabilities belong to S11.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Prompt structure only.
- **Terminology:** Context, template, assumptions, validation.
- **Recommended role:** Prompt Helper backlog after copyright-safe rewriting and review; not a lesson source.

### S8 — PMBOK 8 process presentation

- **File:** `08.2_PMBOK+Process+Presentation.pdf` (348 pages)
- **Purpose:** Detailed encyclopedia for the 40-process model.
- **Major concepts:** Process purpose, recurring inputs/tools/outputs, plans and documents, predictive/adaptive distinctions, scope, schedule, finance, stakeholders, resources, risk, governance, execution, monitoring, change, and closure.
- **Unique material:** Deepest process-level coverage in the library, including process artifacts and relationships.
- **Overlap:** S1 is its compact map; S5 is the domain summary; S6 abstracts common ITTO patterns.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** WBS, schedule networks, PERT, critical path, EVM/forecasting, stakeholder analysis, RACI, risk analysis and response, quality tools, integrated change control.
- **Terminology:** The canonical internal vocabulary for plans, registers, logs, outputs, and process intent—subject to verification against official PMI terminology before publication.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for predictive/process depth and process reference pages; never copied verbatim.

### S9 — Agile mindset and leadership

- **File:** `09_Agile+Mindset+and+leadership.pdf` (127 pages)
- **Purpose:** Agile principles, frameworks, roles, leadership, communication, and team dynamics.
- **Major concepts:** Agile values/principles, Scrum, XP, Lean, Kanban, FDD, Crystal, SAFe, Disciplined Agile, servant leadership, self-organizing teams, stakeholder engagement, facilitation, conflict, motivation, and distributed teams.
- **Unique material:** Broadest agile-framework comparison and strongest people/leadership treatment.
- **Overlap:** Product and flow mechanics in S10; approach selection in S4.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Scrum events/artifacts/accountabilities, Lean wastes, Kanban flow, XP practices, team-development and motivation models.
- **Terminology:** Product Owner, Scrum Master, increment, backlog, WIP, information radiator, psychological safety.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for agile mindset, framework comparison, and servant-leadership lessons.

### S10 — Agile product, delivery, and hybrid

- **File:** `10_Agile+Product,+Delivery+and+Hybrid.pdf` (129 pages)
- **Purpose:** Adaptive product delivery, estimation, flow, value, formulas, risk, improvement, and hybrid integration.
- **Major concepts:** Early value, adaptive planning, chartering, financial metrics, EVM/PERT/channel formulas, MVP/MMF, user stories, INVEST, estimation, prioritization, lead/cycle time, KPIs, burn charts, velocity, technical debt, risk, retrospectives, and hybrid patterns.
- **Unique material:** Connects product/backlog mechanics to quantitative reference material and hybrid delivery patterns.
- **Overlap:** Agile foundations and leadership in S9; schedule/finance/risk processes in S8.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** EVM, PERT, communication channels, Little’s Law, flow metrics, planning poker, value prioritization, retrospective stages.
- **Terminology:** MVP, MMF, user story, Three Cs, INVEST, story points, velocity, throughput, cycle time, lead time.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for agile product/delivery depth, formula sheets, and hybrid integration.

### S11 — AI in project management

- **File:** `11_AI+Slides.pdf` (22 pages)
- **Purpose:** AI adoption, responsible use, exam mindset, and project-management use cases.
- **Major concepts:** Automation, assistance, augmentation, human oversight, bias, privacy, accountability, safety, transparency, copyright, sustainability, reporting, forecasting, scheduling, risk, and stakeholder applications.
- **Unique material:** Only dedicated responsible-AI source and the clearest human-accountability model.
- **Overlap:** Introductory AI vocabulary in S2; prompt patterns in S7.
- **Frameworks/formulas:** Three adoption levels; responsible-use checklist.
- **Terminology:** Automation, assistance, augmentation, bias, explainability, human accountability.
- **Recommended role:** Primary source for a four-unit AI module and responsible-use reference sheet.

## Overlap and authority rules

1. Use S1 and S8 for structural/process naming, with official PMI material required as the final publication check.
2. Use S3 for principle framing and S5 for domain synthesis.
3. Use S6 to teach ITTO patterns; use S8 only for concrete process examples.
4. Use S9 for agile mindset/frameworks and S10 for product, quantitative, flow, and hybrid mechanics.
5. Use S11 for responsible AI; treat S7 only as a prompt-helper pattern.
6. When sources disagree, record the conflict and verify against the current official ECO/standard rather than choosing by repetition.
